About Commissions

About the Project

Commissions for Charity is nonprofit dedicated to helping the homeless population of Austin, Texas.

There are an estimated 2,500 homeless individuals currently living in Austin. This is due to a number of factors including preexisting mental and/or physical health conditions, lack of afforable housing, and past abusive relationships. While homeless, these individuals may face violence, legal issues, and a general lack of basic needs. The goal of this project is to help those affected by homelessness by donating commission proceeds to charities in the Austin Area. By contributing to this project, you will recieve:

  • A one of a kind piece of digital art.
  • A receipt of donation from one of the listed charities.
  • A place for your name on the contributors section listed on the website.

The Data Behind Homelessness

Proposition B

Passed on May 3rd 2021, Proposition B reinstated the public camping ban, making it a Class C misdemeanor for any homeless individual to camp in public areas. This law only exacerbates a homeless person's situation, burdening them with a potential criminal record and a fine they cannot pay.

Racial Disparities

"Black/African American residents represent more than 1 in 3 people in our homeless population, but fewer than 1 in 10 people in the Austin/Travis County population." - Ending Community Homelessness Coalition



Employment Is Not a Quick Fix

An 2009 study by The National Coalition for the Homeless suggests that around 44% percent of the United State's homeless population is employed in full or part time jobs. Furthermore, The National Low Income Housing Coalition suggests a minimum wage worker would need to work 125 hours a week to afford a one-bedroom apartment in Austin.

Supported Charities

Why Donate to Commissions for Charity

Front Steps

Front Steps manages the Austin Resource Center for the Homeless (ARCH) via a contract with the City of Austin.

In addition to shelter services, Front Steps contributes to our community’s goal meeting the permanent supportive housing needs of homeless individuals through the development of three low barrier housing programs.

https://frontsteps.org/

The Other Ones

The Other Ones foundation transitions Austin’s homeless neighbors into an engaged community through shelter, opportunity, and support.

The Other Ones Foundation is a nonprofit that offers extremely low-barrier work opportunities, case management, and humanitarian aid to people experiencing homelessness in Austin, TX.

https://toofound.org

Ending Community Homelessness Coalition INC

"Our Mission is to provide dynamic, proactive leadership that engages policymakers and the community to end homelessness.""

The Ending Community Homelessness Coalition (ECHO) is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization and is the lead agency that plans and implements community-wide strategies to end homelessness in Austin and Travis County. We are the backbone for the homeless services system and work closely with nonprofits and government agencies to coordinate assistance and housing for people experiencing homelessness in our community.

https://www.austinecho.org/

About the Artist

Gage Benham

5 Years Technical Experience in Blender

I am a Computer Engineering student pursuing a Bachelor of Science at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley. I currently work as a Software Engineering intern.

Despite my background in STEM, I have always had a fascination with artwork. Doodling on notebooks or sculpting ornaments, art served as a creative escape.

It was my senior year of highschool that I transitioned to digital artwork. 5 years later, I have not looked back and continue to expand my creative boundaries. In my sophmore year of college, I held a workshop to teach others how to create their own digital artwork. Since then there had been this lingering sense that my art could be used for good.

This project represents a culmination of my passions: sharing my art with others and helping those in need.
